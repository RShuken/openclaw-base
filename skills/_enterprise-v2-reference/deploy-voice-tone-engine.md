## Purpose

Extend {{AGENT_NAME}}'s existing voice system to write in {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}}'s specific voice for all outbound communication — email replies, follow-up drafts, briefing tone, Telegram messages, and action item communications.

**This builds on what already exists.** OpenClaw has a proven three-layer system:

```
Layer 1: IDENTITY.md — WHO the agent is (name, personality, traits)
         Already deployed on {{AGENT_NAME}}. ✓

Layer 2: SOUL.md — HOW the agent communicates (core truths, vibe, style rules)
         Already deployed on {{AGENT_NAME}}. ✓ Needs update for {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}}-specific voice.

Layer 3: Humanizer — POST-PROCESSING cleanup (strips 24+ AI writing patterns)
         Listed as Layer 0.4 in install checklist. Needs deployment.
```

**What this skill ADDS (new):**

```
Layer 4: STYLE.md — {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}}'s SPECIFIC writing voice
         Auto-generated from 500+ sent emails by Opus.
         9 sections: voice principles, vocabulary, punctuation,
         platform differences, quick reactions, rhetorical moves,
         anti-patterns, good examples, never-say list.

Layer 5: EXAMPLES.md — 15-20 CURATED real writing samples
         Organized by register: investor, founder, internal, casual.
         Plus 5-10 anti-examples of what {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}} does NOT sound like.

Layer 6: RECIPIENTS.md — PER-PERSON tone adaptation
         Based on CRM relationship data. Deep relationships get
         slightly more casual. New contacts get professional default.
```

**Why NOT fine-tuning:** Research confirms every commercial tool (Jasper, Copy.ai, Typeface) and every open-source framework uses the same approach: analyze samples → structured reference document → few-shot examples. Nobody fine-tunes for a single person's voice. Prompt engineering is faster to iterate (update the doc, not retrain), supports multiple registers, and works across model providers.

## The Complete Voice Stack

```
IDENTITY.md (WHO {{AGENT_NAME}} is)
       ↓
SOUL.md (HOW {{AGENT_NAME}} communicates in general)
       ↓
STYLE.md (HOW {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}} specifically writes — auto-generated from corpus)
       ↓
EXAMPLES.md (REAL samples of {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}}'s writing by register)
       ↓
RECIPIENTS.md (Per-person tone adaptation from CRM)
       ↓
Draft generated by Claude Opus (with all layers as context)
       ↓
Humanizer post-processor (strips AI tells)
       ↓
{{EA_NAME}} reviews → {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}} sends (or approves for {{AGENT_NAME}} to send)
```

Every outbound skill (email, briefing, action items, Telegram) loads this stack. The stack is just markdown files — no API calls to load, no dependencies, no latency.

### 2b. Opus Corpus Analysis

`voice-analyze.js` sends the corpus to Claude Opus with this prompt:

```
You are analyzing a VC Managing Director's real communications to extract
their writing voice. Produce a STYLE.md with these 9 sections:

1. VOICE PRINCIPLES (3-5 rules that define how this person writes)
2. VOCABULARY (words they actually use + words they NEVER use)
3. PUNCTUATION & FORMATTING (their actual habits, not generic rules)
4. PLATFORM DIFFERENCES (how their voice changes: email vs. Telegram vs. LinkedIn)
5. QUICK REACTIONS (how they say yes, no, delegate, defer — with real examples)
6. RHETORICAL MOVES (how they persuade, challenge, compliment, reject)
7. ANTI-PATTERNS (phrases this person would NEVER use — be specific)
8. GOOD EXAMPLES (10-15 representative samples from the corpus, annotated)
9. NEVER-SAY LIST (explicit banned phrases — both AI tells and personal avoidances)

Be SPECIFIC. "Writes in a professional tone" is useless. "Opens external emails
with 'Hi {name},' (45% of the time), 'Hey {name},' (25%), or no greeting (15%).
Never uses 'Dear' except for formal LP letters (5%)." is useful.

Ground every observation in evidence from the corpus. If you can't find evidence
for a pattern, don't invent it.
```

### 2c. Output: STYLE.md

A structured markdown file at `${WORKSPACE}/STYLE.md`:

```markdown
# {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}} — Writing Voice

## 1. Voice Principles
- Lead with the point. No preamble.
- Short sentences. Average 12 words.
- Action-oriented. Every email has a clear ask or next step.
- Warm but not effusive. Professional but not corporate.
- Opinions are real, not hedged.

## 2. Vocabulary
**Uses:** "Let's do it", "sounds good", "sharp" (about people), "I'm in"
**Never uses:** "utilize" (says "use"), "leverage" (says "use"),
  "circle back" (says "reconnect"), "touch base" (says "catch up"),
  "synergy", "deep dive", "paradigm"

## 3. Punctuation & Formatting
- Periods over em dashes ({{EA_NAME}}: "the formatting is shit" when AI overuses dashes)
- Bullet points for multiple items, numbered lists for action items
- Short paragraphs: 1-3 sentences max
- White space between paragraphs
- Hyperlinks ALWAYS (never raw URLs)
- Full email width (never narrow-column AI formatting)

## 4. Platform Differences
- Email (investor): "Hi Sarah," ... "Best, {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}}"
- Email (founder): "Hey Marcus," ... "— {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}}"
- Email (internal): "{{TEAM_ANALYST}}," ... "Thanks" or nothing
- Telegram: Very brief. No greeting. Straight to content.
- LinkedIn: Slightly more polished. Still direct.

## 5. Quick Reactions
- Yes: "Let's do it." / "I'm in." / "Sounds good — let's move forward."
- No: "Going to pass on this one." / "Not the right fit for us right now."
- Delegate: "Looping in {{TEAM_ANALYST}}." / "{{EA_NAME}} can help coordinate."
- Defer: "Let me think on it." ({{EA_NAME}} note: this usually means no)
- Excited: "Love it." / "This is great."

## 6. Rhetorical Moves
- Compliment + constructive feedback: "Great progress on X. Two things to think about: ..."
- Soft rejection: "Appreciate you thinking of us — going to pass."
- Urgency: "Need this by EOD" (not "at your earliest convenience")
- Introduction: "John, meet Sarah. Sarah runs [company] and is doing [impressive thing]."

## 7. Anti-Patterns ({{PRINCIPAL_NAME}} would NEVER write these)
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "I just wanted to reach out"
- "Per our conversation"
- "As discussed"
- "Please don't hesitate to reach out"
- "I wanted to circle back on"
- Any sentence starting with "I believe that" (he just states the belief)

## 8. Good Examples
[15-20 real excerpts from corpus, organized by register, with annotations]

## 9. Never-Say List
delve, leverage, utilize, robust, seamless, synergy, paradigm,
"it's worth noting", "at the end of the day", "moving forward",
"in terms of", "I hope this finds you", "per our conversation",
"please don't hesitate", "touch base", "circle back", "deep dive",
"double down", "low-hanging fruit", "bandwidth" (ironic given his book title)
```

### 2e. RECIPIENTS.md

Per-person tone adaptation rules, generated from CRM data:

```markdown
# Recipient Tone Rules

## Default by Type
| Recipient Type | Register | Greeting | Sign-off |
|---------------|----------|----------|----------|
| LP/Investor | Professional-warm | "Hi {name}," | "Best, {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}}" |
| Portfolio Founder | Supportive-direct | "Hey {name}," | "— {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}}" |
| Internal (senior) | Brief-action | "{name}," | "Thanks" |
| Internal (team) | Brief-action | "{name}," | nothing |
| Friends/Family | Casual-warm | "Hey!" | "— D" |

## Overrides by Relationship Depth
| Depth | Adjustment |
|-------|-----------|
| DEEP (10+ interactions) | One step more casual than default |
| ACTIVE (3-9 interactions) | Default register |
| WARM (1-2 interactions) | Default register |
| COLD (new contact) | One step more formal than default |

## Specific People (from CRM EA/preference data)
- John Smith (Sequoia): Always CC jane.doe@sequoiacap.com (EA)
- [Other specific rules {{EA_NAME}} maintains]
```

## Phase 3: Review & Approval

1. **Opus generates** STYLE.md, EXAMPLES.md, RECIPIENTS.md from corpus
2. **{{PRINCIPAL_NAME}} reviews** STYLE.md: "Does this sound like me?" Corrects patterns, adds stances, flags wrong phrases.
3. **{{EA_NAME}} reviews** formatting rules, EA CC rules, recipient adaptations, "soft no" patterns
4. **Both approve** → files saved with timestamp
5. **Continuous learning**: every edit {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}}/{{EA_NAME}} makes to a draft feeds back into the voice profile

## Phase 4: Integration with Skills

Every skill that generates outbound text loads the voice stack:

```javascript
// In any skill that drafts text:
const identity = fs.readFileSync(`${WORKSPACE}/IDENTITY.md`, 'utf8');
const soul = fs.readFileSync(`${WORKSPACE}/SOUL.md`, 'utf8');
const style = fs.readFileSync(`${WORKSPACE}/STYLE.md`, 'utf8');
const examples = fs.readFileSync(`${WORKSPACE}/EXAMPLES.md`, 'utf8');

// Build system prompt with voice context
const systemPrompt = `${identity}\n\n${soul}\n\n${style}\n\n${examples}`;

// Per-recipient adaptation
const recipient = lookupRecipient(recipientEmail); // from CRM
const register = getRegister(recipient); // from RECIPIENTS.md rules

// Generate draft
const draft = await opus.generate({
  system: systemPrompt,
  user: `Write a ${register} email to ${recipient.name} about: ${topic}`
});

// Post-process through humanizer
const cleaned = humanizer.process(draft);
```

**Token cost of voice stack:** STYLE.md (~2,000 tokens) + EXAMPLES.md (~3,000 tokens) + SOUL.md (~1,500 tokens) = ~6,500 tokens of context per generation. With Opus at $15/M input tokens, that's ~$0.10 per draft. Acceptable.

## Phase 5: Continuous Learning

| Signal | What Updates | How |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}} edits a draft before sending | The DIFF reveals voice gaps | Logged to `data/voice-corrections.jsonl` |
| {{EA_NAME}} edits a draft | Same — her corrections refine formatting/tone | Same log |
| New sent emails (corpus grows) | Patterns become more confident | Monthly re-analysis by Nightman |
| Explicit corrections ("I don't say 'circle back'") | Added to Never-Say list immediately | Direct STYLE.md update |
| Recipient feedback (how people respond to {{AGENT_NAME}}'s drafts) | Tone calibration per recipient type | Long-term signal, quarterly review |

Every 30 days, Nightman re-analyzes the corpus (including new emails since last analysis) and proposes STYLE.md updates. {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}}/{{EA_NAME}} review the changes.

## Validation

The "would {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}} actually send this?" test:

1. Generate 10 sample drafts across different contexts (investor, founder, internal, casual)
2. {{PRINCIPAL_NAME}} rates each 1-5 on "sounds like me"
3. Target: average score > 4.0
4. Below 3.5 on any individual draft → investigate and update STYLE.md
5. Repeat quarterly

Research-backed KPIs:
- **Voice recognition rate**: 85%+ in blind identification (show someone 5 drafts, can they tell which is AI?)
- **Revision rate**: Under 25% of drafts need voice-related edits
- **Cross-context consistency**: 90%+ alignment on core voice attributes across registers
